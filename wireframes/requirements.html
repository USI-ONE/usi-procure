Requirements & Scope
What we're building, drawn from the scoping meeting. Review this alongside the wireframe and flag anything that's off before we write code.

1 The problem

Purchasing spends significant time comparing price and availability across distributor and reseller sites by hand — copy a SKU, search each site, compare manually. It's repetitive, slow, and makes it hard to consistently land the best buy. Vendors also negotiate: list price online is often $5–$200/unit above what USI actually pays when we ask (ASI, D&H) — roughly 10% of the time worth chasing.

2 Objective

A centralized, web-based purchasing intelligence tool that searches USI's distributor network for a part and returns a consolidated, ranked view of price, availability, and purchasing terms — cutting procurement time and building a foundation for pricing analytics over time.

3 Search & matching

The hard part isn't the results table — it's the search. What we call a part on Amazon isn't what Ingram calls it. Two entry paths:

  • SR-1 SKU / MPN search — exact manufacturer part number lookup across all vendors.
  • SR-2 Natural-language search — e.g. "32GB RAM that fits a Dell Latitude" or "M.2 NVMe for a ThinkPad." The tool interprets intent and finds the matching part per vendor, even when their naming differs. Powered by a hosted LLM (Claude / GPT) — not a model we build.
  • SR-3 Find-once, log-once — once a SKU is identified, save the mapping so future searches are instant.
  • SR-4 Build-fit confirmation (future) — validate that a part actually fits the target platform, supplementing in-house expertise. Possible public-facing consumer version in a later phase.

5 Data captured per vendor

  • DR-1 Current unit price (our buy price where exposed, else list)
  • DR-2 Stock availability & quantity where available
  • DR-3 Vendor name
  • DR-4 Payment terms — Account / Net vs. Credit Card
  • DR-5 Direct link to the product listing
  • DR-6 Timestamp of when pricing was collected

6 MVP deliverables

  • MVP-1 Single search (SKU + natural language) across the four primary vendors
  • MVP-2 Automated, consolidated comparison table
  • MVP-3 Inventory visibility where available
  • MVP-4 Sort & filter by price, availability, vendor, and payment terms
  • MVP-5 Best-price highlight + links back to each vendor listing
  • MVP-6 Log searched SKUs to a catalog

7 Future enhancements

  • Historical price database for every SKU searched + trend visualization
  • Price-drop & inventory alerts; saved watchlists for frequent buys
  • Predictive purchasing insights from historical pricing
  • Quote generation; recommendations by total landed cost, terms & availability
  • "Request a better deal" workflow for negotiable vendors
  • Possible public/consumer-facing search on the USI website (V2)

8 Platform & non-functional

  • NF-1 Web-based, fast & lightweight — usable on a phone, not tethered to a desk. Built on Next.js.
  • NF-2 Hosted on Vercel — internal / not public client-facing in V1; secure without heavy hardening.
  • NF-3 Neon (PostgreSQL) database — no credit-card or client-financial data, so no heavyweight hardening needed. Standard Postgres so it can be replicated to Azure for a sensitive V2.
  • NF-4 Hosted LLM for natural-language search (Claude / GPT) on a subscription tier — no in-house model training. Est. ~$25/mo plan covers expected usage.
  • NF-5 Access-controlled — internal subscription-level access, not open to the public in V1.
